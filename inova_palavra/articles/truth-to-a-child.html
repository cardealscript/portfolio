How to Tell the Truth to a Child

By Cardeal Felisberto | 2026

There are secrets that mothers carry in silence, secrets so heavy that they bend the soul. Among them lies one of the most painful confessions: that at some moment of despair, she attempted to end the life of the very child she now embraces.

This dilemma is the battlefield between conscience and silence. For years, the mother lives with this secret chained within her. Each smile, each achievement of the child carries not only pride, but also the haunting echo of a past decision.

Why do women find themselves in such places? Often, it is not out of hatred, but fear, poverty, or loneliness. A pregnancy can arrive like a storm, and in that fragile moment, a woman may believe that ending it is her only escape.

"Truth is not only a burden to be carried but also a bridge to healing. When a mother finds the courage to reveal the truth, space opens for reconciliation."

Should the truth be told? Speaking means risking rejection, but silence is its own prison. To confess requires rare courage—the courage to be misunderstood in the name of truth. But truth, when spoken with love, has the power to heal.

In the end, what matters most is that love triumphed over despair. The child is here, alive and loved. Telling the truth is not about reliving shame, but about transforming guilt into a testimony of resilience and grace.
